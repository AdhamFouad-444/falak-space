# FALAK Initiative - Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)

**Initiative Overview**
FALAK is a student-led initiative exploring how space education and participation can be made more open, accessible, and practical. Currently in the early research and design phase, FALAK focuses on building a scalable pathway into space â€” from STEM education and rocketry fundamentals to hands-on experimentation and future launch access.

**Mission:** Opening Pathways into Space  
**Status:** Research & Design Phase  
**Core Philosophy:** Progression, not gatekeeping. We believe accessibility drives innovation.  
**Approach:** Leading with inclusivity, enabled by AI, grounded in community.

---

## 1. Initiative Governance & Structure
### 1.1 Leadership & Decision-Making
*   **Student-Led Model:** Core decisions made by founding multidisciplinary student team (Business, Mathematics, Physics, Astrophysics)
*   **Advisory Board:** Quarterly consultations with aerospace professionals, educators, and industry partners
*   **Consensus Building:** Major strategic decisions require 75% team agreement
*   **Documentation:** All decisions documented with rationale for future reference

### 1.2 Team Organization
*   **Core Roles:** Research Lead, Partnership Coordinator, Technical Lead, Communications Lead
*   **Working Groups:** Education, Rocketry, Outreach, Research Development
*   **Meeting Cadence:** Weekly core team meetings, bi-weekly full team assemblies
*   **Onboarding:** New members receive orientation on mission, values, and current projects

### 1.3 Academic Integration
*   **Credit Recognition:** Work with universities to recognize FALAK contributions for academic credit
*   **Faculty Sponsorship:** Maintain relationships with sponsoring faculty members
*   **Research Alignment:** Ensure activities align with academic research standards
*   **Publication Support:** Support team members in publishing research and findings

## 2. Educational Program Development
### 2.1 The 5-Stage Learning Pathway
*   **Stage 1: Spark (Curiosity):** Accessible, high-engagement content to ignite interest.
*   **Stage 2: Understand (Foundation):** Deep conceptual learning of physics and systems.
*   **Stage 3: Build (Hands-On):** Practical application, rocketry construction, and simulation.
*   **Stage 4: Participate (Industry):** Real-world projects, data analysis, and internships.
*   **Stage 5: Lead (Innovation):** Advanced research, mentorship roles, and driving new initiatives.

### 2.2 AI-Enabled Learning Protocols
*   **Curriculum Source:** See [Education Framework 001](./EDUCATION_FRAMEWORK_001.md) for detailed syllabus.
*   **Personalization:** AI tools used to adapt curriculum pace and style to individual learner needs.
*   **Ethics & Quality:** All AI-generated support is verified by subject matter experts.
*   **Enabler, Not Replacement:** AI enhances the learning experience but does not replace human mentorship and community.
*   **Accessibility:** AI translation and simplification tools used to remove language and complexity barriers.

### 2.3 Community-Driven Engagement
*   **Safe Spaces:** Events designed to be welcoming to non-engineers and newcomers.
*   **Barrier Removal:** Active identification and removal of financial, social, or academic gates.
*   **Mentorship Circles:** Peer-to-peer learning encouraged alongside expert guidance.
*   **Open Resources:** All educational materials made freely available under Creative Commons.

## 3. Rocketry Program Operations
### 3.1 Safety Standards
*   **Primary Directive:** Safety is non-negotiable and supersedes all other considerations
*   **Training Required:** All participants complete safety certification before handling materials
*   **Supervision Ratios:** Minimum 1 qualified supervisor per 5 participants for practical work
*   **Equipment Inspections:** All rockets and equipment inspected before each use
*   **Launch Protocols:** Detailed checklists for pre-launch, launch, and recovery procedures
*   **Incident Reporting:** All safety incidents documented and reviewed within 24 hours

### 3.2 Progressive Skill Development
*   **Level 1:** Model rocket construction using pre-manufactured kits
*   **Level 2:** Custom rocket design using standard motors and materials
*   **Level 3:** Advanced designs with data collection and telemetry
*   **Level 4:** High-powered rocketry with experimental payloads
*   **Certification:** Participants advance through levels based on demonstrated competency

### 3.3 Launch Operations
*   **Site Selection:** Launches conducted only at approved, legal locations
*   **Regulatory Compliance:** All activities comply with national aviation and safety regulations
*   **Weather Assessment:** Launches canceled if wind exceeds 20 mph or conditions unsafe
*   **Range Safety:** Minimum safe distances maintained for all spectators and operators
*   **Data Collection:** Flight data recorded for analysis and educational purposes
*   **Environmental Care:** All launch sites left cleaner than found, debris recovered

### 3.4 Equipment Management
*   **Inventory System:** All equipment cataloged with condition assessments
*   **Maintenance Schedule:** Regular inspection and maintenance of reusable components
*   **Storage Protocols:** Proper storage conditions for motors, electronics, and materials
*   **Borrowing System:** Clear checkout/return procedures for shared equipment
*   **Budget Tracking:** All purchases documented and tracked against project budgets

## 4. Research & Development Framework
### 4.1 Project Initiation
*   **Proposal Process:** All research projects begin with written proposal including objectives, methodology, timeline
*   **Feasibility Review:** Team reviews technical feasibility and resource requirements
*   **Ethics Consideration:** Projects assessed for safety, environmental, and ethical implications
*   **Approval Requirements:** Projects approved by both student leadership and faculty advisor

### 4.2 Experimental Protocols
*   **Documentation Standards:** All experiments follow scientific method with detailed documentation
*   **Data Management:** Raw data stored in shared repository with backup procedures
*   **Peer Review:** Findings reviewed by team members before external presentation
*   **Publication Path:** Support for converting strong projects into papers or presentations
*   **Open Science:** Default to open sharing of methodologies and findings

### 4.3 Collaboration & Partnerships
*   **Research Networks:** Build connections with other student aerospace groups
*   **Industry Engagement:** Seek mentorship and guidance from aerospace professionals
*   **University Collaboration:** Partner with university labs for access to facilities and expertise
*   **Conference Participation:** Present work at student aerospace conferences when possible

## 5. Responsible Development Principles
### 5.1 Safety Culture
*   **Risk Assessment:** All activities undergo formal risk assessment before execution
*   **Training Requirements:** Participants trained on specific risks and mitigation strategies
*   **Emergency Preparedness:** First aid kits, emergency contacts, and evacuation plans for all events
*   **Insurance Coverage:** Appropriate liability insurance maintained for organizational activities
*   **Continuous Improvement:** Safety protocols updated based on incidents and near-misses

### 5.2 Environmental Responsibility
*   **Sustainable Practices:** Minimize waste and environmental impact in all activities
*   **Site Restoration:** Leave all outdoor sites in better condition than found
*   **Material Selection:** Prefer reusable and recyclable materials when possible
*   **Pollution Prevention:** Proper disposal of all chemicals, electronics, and hazardous materials
*   **Education:** Teach participants about aerospace industry's environmental considerations

### 5.3 Ethical Considerations
*   **Dual-Use Awareness:** Conscious of potential military or harmful applications of technology
*   **Inclusive Access:** Actively work to remove financial and social barriers to participation
*   **Cultural Sensitivity:** Respect diverse backgrounds and perspectives in all activities
*   **Transparency:** Open about limitations, failures, and challenges
*   **Intellectual Property:** Clear policies on ownership and sharing of designs and innovations

## 6. Partnership & Stakeholder Management
### 6.1 Educational Institution Partnerships
*   **Relationship Building:** Regular communication and updates to partner institutions
*   **Value Demonstration:** Track and report on student engagement and learning outcomes
*   **Feedback Integration:** Actively incorporate partner feedback into program design
*   **Mutual Benefit:** Ensure partnerships benefit both FALAK and partner organizations
*   **Formal Agreements:** Document expectations and commitments in MOUs when appropriate

### 6.2 Industry Engagement
*   **Mentorship Programs:** Connect students with aerospace professionals for guidance
*   **Site Visits:** Organize tours of aerospace facilities when possible
*   **Guest Speakers:** Invite industry experts for talks and Q&A sessions
*   **Internship Pathways:** Help connect advanced students with internship opportunities
*   **Sponsorship Relations:** Cultivate relationships with potential equipment or funding sponsors

### 6.3 Regional Space Ecosystem Integration
*   **Ecosystem Mapping:** Maintain awareness of regional space organizations and initiatives
*   **Collaborative Opportunities:** Seek joint projects or events with aligned organizations
*   **Knowledge Sharing:** Contribute to regional aerospace community through presentations and reports
*   **Resource Pooling:** Explore opportunities to share facilities, equipment, or expertise
*   **Advocacy Support:** Support regional efforts to develop space sector capabilities

## 7. Communications & Outreach
### 7.1 Public Communication
*   **Social Media:** Regular updates on LinkedIn and other platforms about activities and progress
*   **Website Maintenance:** Keep website current with latest information and opportunities
*   **Professional Tone:** All external communications reviewed for professionalism and accuracy
*   **Visual Documentation:** High-quality photos and videos of activities for outreach
*   **Success Stories:** Share achievements and milestones to build credibility

### 7.2 Recruitment & Engagement
*   **Campus Presence:** Regular information sessions at partner universities
*   **Open Events:** Monthly open meetings for interested newcomers
*   **Skill Diversity:** Welcome students from all disciplines, not just engineering
*   **Onboarding Support:** Pair new members with experienced team members as mentors
*   **Retention Focus:** Regular check-ins to ensure members feel engaged and valued

### 7.3 Media Relations
*   **Press Releases:** Announce major milestones and successful launches
*   **Interview Preparation:** Brief team members before any media interactions
*   **Fact-Checking:** Ensure all claims in media materials are accurate and verifiable
*   **Photo Permissions:** Obtain consent before using photos of identifiable individuals
*   **Crisis Communication:** Designated spokesperson for handling any negative situations

## 8. Financial Management
### 8.1 Funding Sources
*   **University Funding:** Apply for student organization grants and project funding
*   **Sponsorships:** Seek in-kind or financial support from aerospace companies
*   **Crowdfunding:** Use for specific projects with clear goals and timelines
*   **Grant Applications:** Pursue educational and research grants aligned with mission
*   **Membership Dues:** Minimal dues to create sustainability (with waivers available)

### 8.2 Budget Management
*   **Transparent Tracking:** All income and expenses documented in shared spreadsheet
*   **Approval Process:** Expenses over threshold require leadership team approval
*   **Receipt Collection:** All purchases backed by receipts and documentation
*   **Quarterly Reviews:** Financial status reviewed with full team quarterly
*   **Reserve Fund:** Maintain minimum reserve for emergency needs or opportunities

### 8.3 Resource Allocation
*   **Project-Based Budgets:** Each major project has dedicated budget allocation
*   **Prioritization:** Resources allocated based on educational impact and feasibility
*   **Cost Efficiency:** Seek donated materials and low-cost alternatives when possible
*   **Equipment Investment:** Balance single-use consumables with durable equipment purchases

## 9. Risk Management & Contingency Planning
### 9.1 Risk Categories
*   **Safety Risks:** Physical harm to participants or public
*   **Financial Risks:** Overspending, loss of funding, equipment damage
*   **Reputational Risks:** Negative publicity, failed launches, safety incidents
*   **Partnership Risks:** Loss of institutional support or partnerships
*   **Regulatory Risks:** Non-compliance with aviation or safety regulations

### 9.2 Mitigation Strategies
*   **Risk Register:** Maintain document of identified risks with mitigation plans
*   **Insurance Coverage:** Appropriate liability and property insurance for activities
*   **Legal Review:** Seek legal advice for agreements and high-risk activities
*   **Backup Plans:** Alternative plans for launches, events, and critical activities
*   **Regular Review:** Quarterly risk assessment reviews by leadership team

### 9.3 Incident Response
*   **Immediate Response:** Clear protocols for handling emergencies during activities
*   **Communication Plan:** Who communicates what to whom in various scenarios
*   **Documentation:** Thorough documentation of any incidents for learning and legal purposes
*   **Stakeholder Notification:** Timely, honest communication with partners and supporters
*   **Corrective Actions:** Implementation of lessons learned to prevent recurrence

## 10. Progress Tracking & Evaluation
### 10.1 Metrics & Milestones
*   **Participant Numbers:** Track students reached through programs and events
*   **Launch Success Rate:** Document successful vs. unsuccessful rocket launches
*   **Partnership Growth:** Number and quality of institutional partnerships
*   **Research Output:** Papers, presentations, and projects completed
*   **Educational Impact:** Pre/post assessments of participant learning when possible

### 10.2 Documentation & Reporting
*   **Project Logs:** All projects maintain ongoing documentation of progress
*   **Monthly Reports:** Leadership team produces monthly summary for team and advisors
*   **Annual Review:** Comprehensive annual report on achievements, challenges, learnings
*   **Photo/Video Archive:** Organized collection of media from all activities
*   **Lessons Learned:** Regular capture of what worked and what didn't

### 10.3 Continuous Improvement
*   **Retrospectives:** After-action reviews for all major projects and events
*   **Feedback Collection:** Regular surveys and discussions with participants
*   **Benchmark Learning:** Study successful aerospace education programs elsewhere
*   **Iterative Development:** Use data and feedback to refine approaches continuously
*   **Innovation Encouragement:** Support team members in proposing and testing new ideas

## 11. Long-Term Vision & Scaling
### 11.1 Phase 1 (Current): Foundation Building
*   Establish core team and governance structure
*   Develop initial educational content and rocketry curriculum
*   Build partnerships with 2-3 educational institutions
*   Conduct first successful launches and educational workshops
*   Establish safety protocols and operational procedures

### 11.2 Phase 2 (12-24 months): Expansion & Refinement
*   Scale programs to 5+ partner institutions
*   Develop advanced rocketry capabilities and experimental payloads
*   Publish research findings and educational resources
*   Establish sustainable funding model
*   Build regional network of collaborators

### 11.3 Phase 3 (24-36 months): Maturation & Impact
*   Program adoption by multiple schools and universities
*   Advanced projects with data collection and analysis
*   Established track record of successful launches and research
*   Strong brand recognition in regional space ecosystem
*   Clear pathways for students from education to careers or advanced studies

### 11.4 Future Considerations
*   Potential formalization as non-profit organization
*   Development of advanced capabilities (high altitude, experimental payloads)
*   International collaborations and exchange programs
*   Creation of alumni network and mentorship programs
*   Exploration of launch access pathways as technology and regulations evolve
